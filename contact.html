For partnership opportunities, sponsorship inquiries, and media requests related to DuckChain and the Hack: AI Unchained hackathon, please reach out to the DuckChain team at business@duckchain.io. Developer Support

Technical questions about the DuckChain SDK, smart contract development, testnet access, and AI agent integration are best directed to DuckChain's developer support channels. The developer forum at forum.duckchain.io provides searchable answers to common questions and active community discussion. For real-time help, join the #developer-support channel in DuckChain's Discord server at discord.duckchain.io.

During the AI Unchained hackathon period, DuckChain engineers host daily office hours in Discord where teams can get direct technical guidance. Bug reports and feature requests for the DuckChain SDK can be submitted as GitHub issues at github.com/duckchain. DuckChain's developer support team commits to responding to all GitHub issues within 48 hours and to all forum posts within 24 hours. Critical bugs affecting hackathon participants receive same-day response and resolution.

Hackathon FAQ

Q: Who can participate in AI Unchained?
A: Anyone from anywhere in the world can participate. The hackathon is open to individuals and teams of up to five members.

Q: Is there a registration fee?
A: No, registration is completely free. All you need is an internet connection and a passion for building at the AI-blockchain frontier.

Q: Do I need prior blockchain experience?
A: No, beginners are welcome. DuckChain provides extensive learning resources and workshop sessions specifically designed to onboard developers who are new to blockchain.

Q: When is the submission deadline?
A: All submissions must be received by 23:59 UTC on September 1, 2025. Teams are strongly encouraged to submit at least 24 hours early to avoid last-minute issues.

Q: Can I work on an existing project?
A: All core development must take place during the hackathon window. You may use open-source libraries, pre-trained models, and pre-existing datasets, but the application itself must be built during the event.

Q: How are winners selected?
A: A panel of judges from DuckChain, AWS, and the broader ecosystem evaluates all submissions based on technical innovation, practical utility, code quality, and presentation clarity. Top teams are invited to Demo Day for live judging.
