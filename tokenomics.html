DUCK Token Overview

The DUCK token is the native utility token of the DuckChain network, serving as the fundamental unit of economic value within the ecosystem. DUCK tokens are used to pay for all network services including transaction fees, AI inference costs, smart contract execution, and data storage. The total supply of DUCK is fixed at one billion tokens (1,000,000,000 DUCK), ensuring predictable scarcity and long-term value appreciation as network demand grows.

DUCK tokens are held by network validators, ecosystem contributors, development team, investors, and the community treasury. The token is listed on major cryptocurrency exchanges and can be acquired through trading, staking rewards, ecosystem grants, and hackathon prize distributions. DuckChain's fixed token supply policy ensures that there is no hidden inflation that dilutes existing holders — all token distributions come from pre-allocated pools with transparent vesting schedules and public disclosure of all significant token movements.

Token Utility

DUCK tokens enable participation in every aspect of the DuckChain ecosystem. As a gas token, DUCK is required to pay for all transaction fees on the DuckChain network — from simple token transfers to complex AI agent operations. As a staking token, DUCK can be delegated to validators to earn a share of block rewards and network fees.

As a governance token, DUCK holders can vote on protocol upgrades, parameter changes, and treasury spending proposals through DuckChain's on-chain governance system. As an AI compute payment token, DUCK is used to pay for access to AI model inference endpoints registered on DuckChain's decentralized AI marketplace, creating a circular economy where AI usage drives token demand. This multi-faceted utility design ensures that DUCK tokens are genuinely useful across the full lifecycle of building, deploying, and operating AI-blockchain applications on DuckChain.

Staking & Rewards

DUCK staking is the primary mechanism by which token holders contribute to network security while earning yield on their holdings. Validators must stake a minimum of 100,000 DUCK to participate in block production and earn block rewards. Delegators can stake any amount of DUCK with their preferred validators and earn a proportional share of rewards minus a validator commission.

The current base staking APY is 12% annually, distributed in DUCK tokens from the network inflation pool. Additional staking rewards are available to validators and delegators who participate in governance, contribute to ecosystem development, and maintain high uptime. Unstaking requires a 21-day unbonding period to maintain network stability and prevent rapid capital flight. This unbonding period is a fundamental security mechanism — it ensures that even in periods of market stress, the validator set remains stable and the network continues to operate reliably.

Ecosystem Fund

DuckChain maintains a dedicated Ecosystem Fund comprising 15% of the total DUCK supply (150,000,000 DUCK) reserved for grants, hackathon prizes, developer incentives, and ecosystem growth initiatives. The Ecosystem Fund is governed by DuckChain's DAO, with DUCK holders voting on fund allocations for major initiatives.

The AI Unchained hackathon prize pool — including $1,000,000 in DUCK liquidity support and $100,000 in DUCK rewards — is drawn from the Ecosystem Fund, reflecting the community's commitment to investing in developer talent and innovation. Beyond hackathon prizes, the fund supports ongoing grants for open-source contributors, integration bounties for protocols that build on DuckChain, and educational initiatives that grow the global pool of DuckChain developers. All Ecosystem Fund expenditures are voted on by DUCK holders and executed transparently on-chain, ensuring that the community maintains democratic control over how shared resources are deployed.

Hackathon prize pool: The $1.1M+ AI Unchained prize pool represents the single largest allocation from the Ecosystem Fund in DuckChain's history, reflecting the community's belief that investing in developer talent is the highest-return use of ecosystem resources.
