Hackathon Tracks

Four challenge categories designed to push the boundaries of AI × blockchain innovation

Track 1: AI Agents on Chain

$400,000 DUCK Autonomous Systems On-chain Intelligence

The AI Agents on Chain track challenges participants to build truly autonomous agents that can operate independently on the DuckChain blockchain. A qualifying project in this track must demonstrate an AI agent that can perceive on-chain state, make intelligent decisions based on that state, and execute transactions without human intervention.

Winning projects in this category will show clear evidence of real autonomy — the agent should be able to run for extended periods without manual intervention, handle edge cases gracefully, and demonstrate measurable performance advantages over non-AI alternatives. Judges will evaluate AI sophistication, on-chain integration depth, security practices, and practical utility. Successful examples include AI treasury managers, autonomous trading agents, on-chain governance assistants, and self-healing smart contract systems that automatically detect and respond to anomalous conditions.

Track 2: DeFAI Applications

$300,000 DUCK DeFi + AI Financial Intelligence

The DeFAI Applications track invites builders to reimagine decentralized finance through the lens of artificial intelligence. Projects in this category should demonstrate how AI can make DeFi protocols smarter, safer, or more accessible to a broader audience.

AI-powered risk assessment models that evaluate lending positions in real time, dynamic automated market makers that adapt their pricing curves based on market conditions, AI-driven yield optimizers that allocate liquidity across protocols, and intelligent liquidation bots that minimize market impact — all of these represent the kind of innovation that the DeFAI track is designed to reward. Judges will look for projects that combine technical depth with genuine market utility, demonstrating a clear path from hackathon prototype to production deployment that could realistically attract significant TVL and user adoption.

Track 3: Infrastructure & Tooling

$200,000 DUCK Developer Tools Core Infrastructure

Strong ecosystems are built on strong infrastructure. The Infrastructure & Tooling track recognizes that many of the most impactful contributions to the AI-blockchain space will not be end-user applications but foundational components that enable the entire ecosystem.

Projects in this track might include AI-enhanced oracle networks that provide more accurate and manipulation-resistant price feeds, cross-chain bridges that use AI to optimize routing and minimize slippage, developer tooling that makes it easier to build AI-integrated smart contracts, or monitoring and security tools that use AI to detect and prevent smart contract exploits. Judges will evaluate the technical sophistication of infrastructure solutions and their potential impact on the broader DuckChain developer community. The best infrastructure projects will be ones that, once built, every other team in the ecosystem will want to use.

Track 4: Creative & Open Innovation

$100,000 DUCK Wild Card Open Category

Some of the most transformative applications of technology emerge from unexpected combinations and unconventional thinking. The Creative & Open Innovation track is deliberately open-ended, welcoming any project that uses AI and blockchain in a novel way that does not fit neatly into the other three tracks.

This is the home for bold experiments, unusual applications, and genuinely surprising uses of decentralized AI. Previous examples from similar hackathons have included AI-powered NFT ecosystems where tokens evolve based on holder behavior, decentralized AI art generation markets, on-chain social networks with AI moderation, and AI-driven prediction markets that create their own questions. Let your imagination lead — the judges in this track are looking for projects that make them think about the future differently. The winning project in this category will be something that no one on the judging panel anticipated.
