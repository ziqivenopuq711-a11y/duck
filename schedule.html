Registration Phase

The registration phase for AI Unchained opens on August 1, 2025, allowing interested participants to secure their spots well in advance of the hackathon kickoff. Registration is free and open to individuals and teams from anywhere in the world. Participants register through the official DoraHacks portal, where they can also browse project ideas, connect with potential teammates, and access pre-hackathon learning resources.

During the registration phase, DuckChain will host a series of informational webinars covering the hackathon structure, evaluation criteria, and available technical resources. These sessions are recorded and made available on-demand for participants in different time zones. Teams are encouraged to form and begin ideating during the registration period, though all code must be written during the official hackathon window. DuckChain's pre-hackathon workshops on smart contract security, AI agent design patterns, and DuckChain SDK fundamentals are highly recommended for all participants.

Building Phase

The building phase runs from August 25 to August 31, 2025 — seven intensive days of development, experimentation, and iteration. During this period, participants have access to a dedicated Discord server with channels for each track, direct access to DuckChain and AWS engineers for technical support, and a schedule of daily workshops covering topics from smart contract security to AI agent design patterns.

Midpoint check-ins on August 28 give teams an opportunity to get early feedback from mentors and course-correct if needed. Teams are encouraged to build in public, sharing progress updates in the community channels to build momentum and attract early user feedback. The building phase concludes with a 48-hour sprint as teams finalize their projects and prepare their submissions. During the final 48 hours, the DuckChain engineering team maintains round-the-clock support availability in Discord to help teams resolve last-minute technical issues and optimize their deployments.

Submission Requirements

All project submissions must be finalized by 23:59 UTC on September 1, 2025. Late submissions will not be accepted under any circumstances. A complete submission must include a working demo or deployed application, a GitHub repository with clean, well-documented code, a project description of at least 500 words explaining the problem, solution, and technical architecture, a 3-minute video demo, and all relevant smart contract addresses on the DuckChain testnet or mainnet.

Teams are strongly advised to submit at least 24 hours before the deadline to avoid last-minute technical issues. Submissions are made through the DoraHacks portal and reviewed for completeness before being passed to the judging panel. Incomplete submissions — those missing required components — will not be passed to judges, so teams should carefully review the submission checklist before clicking submit.

Demo Day & Awards

Demo Day takes place on September 5, 2025, giving top teams the opportunity to present their projects live to a panel of judges drawn from DuckChain's core team, AWS, and the broader ecosystem. Each team receives a 10-minute slot: 7 minutes for the demo and 3 minutes for Q&A.

Judges evaluate projects on technical innovation, practical utility, code quality, presentation clarity, and potential for real-world impact. Winners are announced at the AI Unchained Awards Ceremony on September 5, 2025, broadcast live on DuckChain's YouTube channel and Twitter/X spaces. All winning projects receive their prizes within 14 business days of the awards ceremony. Demo Day is open to the public — anyone can watch the live stream and vote for their favorite projects in the community choice award category.
